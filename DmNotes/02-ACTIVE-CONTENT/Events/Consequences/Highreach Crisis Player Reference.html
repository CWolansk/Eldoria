Highreach Crisis — Player Reference

One-page guide for your actions, movement, threats, and goals.

Objective & Clock

  • 10 rounds total. When the clock ends, the city teeters on collapse.
  • Keep at least half of the zones from becoming Lost to save Highreach.
  • Each player gets 2 actions per round.

Round Flow

  1. Each player takes 2 actions (any mix):
    • Move (see Movement)
    • Do a Thing (Douse Fire, Stop Looters, Fight Rats, Close Gate, Escort)
    • Assist (grant an ally advantage on a check)
    • Other (heal, cast, scout, etc.)
  2. Table resolves a short Random Event.
  3. Threats escalate at end of round.

Movement

  • Attempt to move into an adjacent zone.
  • If the destination has no threats (no F/L/R), you arrive automatically.
  • If the destination has any threats or you are Escorting, something may get in your way — expect a quick obstacle check.
  • Zones with 3+ F/L/R are Lost and very dangerous to enter.

Threat Tokens & How to Deal With Them

F
Fire — Douse Fires
  • Recruit helpers: Charisma DC 10/15/20 → gain 1/2/3 people.
  • Each helper and PC adds 1d6 to a water pool.
  • When you Douse: roll the pool — 15+ remove 1 Fire; 25+ remove all Fire in the zone.
  • Water/Fire control magic can help.
L
Looters — Chase & Capture
  • Capture on 2 successes; they escape on 3 failures.
  • Expect quick obstacles (Acrobatics, Athletics, Perception, etc.).
R
Rats — Fight / Hold the Line
  • On a successful hit against a Rat token, remove it from play.
  • Fewer Rats in a Sewer zone makes closing its Gate easier.
Gate
Sewer Gates — Close the Gate
  • Close with 2 successful Athletics checks.
  • More Rats in that zone makes it harder.
  • A teammate can Help to grant advantage.
E
Evac — Escort Civilians
  • Lead groups to the Castle.
  • Escorts move more cautiously and can be slowed by hazards.
